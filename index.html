Accessibile Design

This assignment made me re-adjust how I configure my designs. Typically, I lean towards light colors and fonts that are in cursive or very fined lined. However, those elements are not accessible for those who are visually impaired, the colors tend to bleed into one another if a person is color blind and the font makes it very hard to read because of how the letters flow into eachother in cursive fonts. In this graphic, I used LEXEND, which is the font that is used for those who have dyselxia, as well as a white background and bright red/pink font color. Creating this design opened my mind on how I can be creating graphics, flyers, and posters that are both accessible and still have my personal design touch to them.
